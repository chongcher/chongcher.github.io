I recently had the privilege of joining a work trip to Kuala Lumpur, where I met with representatives from the Legal Profession Qualifying Board Malaysia ("LPQB"), the Malaysian Bar, Christopher & Lee Ong (a member firm of the Rajah & Tann Asia network), and the University of Malaya.

It was a valuable opportunity to engage in discussions on legal training frameworks and the evolving needs of the legal profession in both jurisdictions. These conversations provided meaningful insights into how legal education is structured in Malaysia, from the early stages of training for pupils to the continuing professional development needs and opportunities available to qualified lawyers.

The experience not only deepened my understanding of legal education and professional learning in Malaysia, but also helped strengthen connections and foster the exchange of ideas between our institutions.
